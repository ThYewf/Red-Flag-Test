Spot the Warning Signs

Online predators are skilled manipulators. Learning their tactics can protect you and your friends. Here's what to watch out for:

🚩 Love Bombing

Predators shower you with attention, compliments, and gifts to make you feel special quickly.

🚩 Isolation

They subtly convince you to pull away from trusted friends and family, so they become your only influence.

🚩 Fake Identity

They pretend to be your age, even using stolen pictures or AI-generated avatars to deceive you.

🚩 Gradual Inappropriate Talk

Starting with harmless conversation, then introducing sexual content slowly to test your limits.

🚩 Secrecy Requests

They ask you to hide your conversations, delete messages, or not tell anyone about your chats.

🚩 Guilt & Threats

They manipulate with phrases like “You don’t love me” or threaten to share private content.

🚩 Flattery About Secrets

They praise you for keeping secrets, making it feel like a game or something special.

🚩 Emotional Dependence

They make you feel like you're the only one who understands them and vice versa.

🚩 Urgency to Meet

They constantly push to meet in real life or pressure you to turn on your camera.

🚩 Asking for Inappropriate Content

They eventually ask for photos or videos that make you uncomfortable or are explicit.

🚩 Mirroring Your Interests

They seem to have all the same interests and hobbies, but only to build quick trust.

🚩 Playing the Victim

They guilt trip you by acting like their life is miserable and only you can help them feel better.

👮‍♂️ What the Law Says

  • Sextortion and blackmail using private images is a criminal offense under the Akta Komunikasi dan Multimedia 1998 (Communications and Multimedia Act 1998).
  • Impersonating someone online can be charged under cyber fraud or identity theft laws.
  • Grooming minors online is a punishable offense under the Akta Kesalahan Seksual Terhadap Kanak-Kanak 2017 (SOAC Act 2017).
